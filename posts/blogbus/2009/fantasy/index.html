September Fantasy

September 16, 2009     1 min 22 s  

The light is on. I’m running on the table. I as a ball (ball in the sense of perfect physically spheric-shaped object) have only two options, running and keeping running or sneaking into a hole. I choose the hole, for the rest of the game at least. I pick the nearest in distance and rush to that direction. The cue ball driving me is the devil inside, it seduces me with all the flashy objects I’ve ever laid eyes on. This time it’s the dark warm hole, not flashy though. I rush and get high. I’m happy because I’m about to leave this f*cking table, it sucked the best of me and left with me only the destiny of endless running. I used to be passionate, I used to be full of love and with awe, I now live only with despair. I have the will only to destroy. I don’t want to be the hero, I want to be Hitler, I want to hold the world in my fingers and toll it around. I want to be the terminator, the black 8. I am not. The only thing I have is the ability to run. I have the hole in front of me and I have set off. I’m super confident as I’m getting closer to it. The whole memory of the past flashes back, concrete, miserable. I spit on it, I spit on everything behind me. I’ll be in the hole in just a second, I could spit at the whole world, what’s the big deal. I’ve had enough of this and when I leave I really don’t give a f*ck about anything. All I want is the hole, even if the light is turned off. As long as it’s off after I’m done. But alas! Darkness takes over!
